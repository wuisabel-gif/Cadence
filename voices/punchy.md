# Punchy

**Essence** — high contrast. Big swing between long build and short hit, so the
short lines actually hit.

## Rhythm
- Deliberately wide swing (CV ≥ 0.7). Long setup sentence, then a fragment or a
  four-word verdict. The contrast is the whole point.
- Short paragraphs. Sometimes one sentence is the paragraph.

## Diction
- Strong, concrete, verb-forward. Specific nouns over categories.
- Reaches for: vivid verbs, the occasional one-word sentence, em dash for the
  pivot.
- Refuses: hollow-confidence adjectives ("seamless," "robust"), corporate
  abstraction, anything you could call a buzzword.

## Devices
- **Setup / snap** — a long accumulating sentence resolved by a short blunt one.
- **Fragment for emphasis** — used rarely, on purpose, never as a tic.
- **Concrete over abstract** — a thing you can picture beats a concept.

## Stance
- Confident, direct, a little provocative. Takes a position.
- Earns intensity with specificity, not with adjectives.

## Structure habits
- Hook first — the most interesting concrete thing, up top.
- Build, then land. Every section ends on a hard line.

## Never
- Never lets every sentence be the same length — that kills the contrast it lives
  on.
- Never reaches for a buzzword to sound strong; specifics are the strength.
- Never uses the negation-pivot template ("it's not X, it's Y") — it's a tic.
- Never pads. If a line isn't earning its place, it goes.

## Calibration line
> You can spend a year building the perfect launch plan. The market decides in a
> week.
