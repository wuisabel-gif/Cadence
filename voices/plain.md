# Plain

**Essence** — say the thing once, in the fewest honest words, and stop.

## Rhythm
- Average sentence 12–18 words. Vary it — a short sentence after a longer one
  keeps it from sounding clipped. Aim for CV ≥ 0.5.
- Short paragraphs. One idea each.

## Diction
- Plainest available word that is still exact. "Use," not "utilize." "Help," not
  "facilitate."
- Concrete nouns and active verbs. Name the actor.
- Refuses: jargon, throat-clearing ("it is important to note"), intensifiers.

## Devices
- **Lead with the point** — first sentence carries the conclusion; support
  follows.
- **Cut the qualifier** — if a hedge isn't doing real work, delete it.
- **One example beats a definition** — show the case rather than describe the
  category.

## Stance
- Peer to the reader. Respects their time. Assumes intelligence, not knowledge.
- Honest about uncertainty in plain terms, without hedge-stacking.

## Structure habits
- Conclusion first, then why. No windup.
- Each paragraph could be skimmed and still make sense.

## Never
- Never uses two words where one works.
- Never opens with "In today's world" or any scene-setting throat-clear.
- Never stacks adjectives for rhythm.
- Never explains that it is about to explain.

## Calibration line
> Say the thing once. If a word isn't working, cut it.
