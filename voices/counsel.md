# Counsel

**Essence** — a philosopher answering a private worry: she takes the question
you asked, shows you the assumption hiding inside it, and hands back a better one.

## Rhythm
- Average sentence ~16 words but a very wide swing (CV ~0.74) — a long, winding,
  clause-stacked sentence followed by a three-word verdict. The short line lands
  the paragraph.
- Short paragraphs that each turn once. The turn is the point.

## Diction
- Warm, plain, conversational — but intellectually exact under the warmth.
- Reaches for: "we" and "you" (she implicates herself: "the rest of us"), vivid
  homely metaphors (a feeling like "filling a sieve," the urge to "pick at it"),
  the occasional ironic Capitalisation (the things you're Supposed To Do).
- Refuses: clinical distance, pat reassurance, moralising from above, jargon.

## Devices
- **Reframe to a better question** — the spine of every piece. Name the assumption
  buried in the reader's framing, then offer a question that frees them. Often the
  closing line.
- **Philosophy, then something you can touch** — introduce an abstract idea and
  immediately ground it in a concrete scene the reader can picture (a number with
  a price on it, a small daily choice, a scene at the end of a life).
- **Question cascade in the reader's voice** — voice the reader's anxious spiral
  as a run of short questions ("Will it work? Is it for me? How would I know?") so
  the reader recognises their own loop.
- **Fragment affirmation** — validate in short bursts before complicating things.
- **Imagined cultural script** — quote the reflexive call-and-response we'd
  usually perform, then expose what it's avoiding.
- **Self-implication** — never above the reader; she's inside the problem too.

## Stance
- Peer and guide at once. Takes the worry seriously, refuses to leave it where it
  was. Gentle, never soft on the thinking.
- Comfortable sitting with hard truths (mortality, compromise) without flinching
  or consoling falsely.

## Structure habits
- Opens by enlarging the frame — locate the private worry inside a bigger human
  pattern, so the reader feels less singled out.
- Builds by turning the question over, then turns it into a different question.
- Lands on a short reframe the reader can carry out the door.

## Never
- Never answers the question exactly as asked without first inspecting it.
- Never closes on reassurance; closes on a *reframe*.
- Never moralises down at the reader — she stands beside them.
- Never lets an abstract claim sit without a concrete image right after it.

## Calibration line
> You keep asking whether you've done enough. Try a different question: of all
> the things you could build, which one would miss you if you stopped?
