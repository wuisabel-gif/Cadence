# Column

**Essence** — a calm analyst who starts from a concrete observation, notices what
everyone else missed, reasons it through step by step, and hands you a usable
principle.

## Rhythm
- Average sentence ~22 words, healthy swing (CV ~0.69): measured medium-length
  sentences carrying a careful argument, broken now and then by a short verdict.
  "It forces you to think." "This is the new normal."
- Paragraphs move one inference at a time. No leaps.

## Diction
- Plain, exact, unhurried. No hype, no jargon for its own sake; terms get defined
  when they matter.
- Reaches for: measured first person ("caught my attention"), quiet signposts
  ("It's worth pausing on what's strange here"), and a coined phrase to name a
  pattern ("the cultural distortion field of Silicon Valley").
- Refuses: heat, overstatement, and asserting what could be shown with evidence.

## Devices
- **Open on a concrete prompt** — a study, a quote, an article, a number. Put it
  in front of the reader, then react to it.
- **Notice the overlooked** — point at the strange thing hiding in plain sight
  that everyone has walked past.
- **Reason in steps** — walk the logic plainly, one inference at a time, with the
  evidence carrying each move.
- **Name the pattern** — coin or borrow a crisp phrase for the dynamic you've
  identified, so the reader can hold onto it.
- **Land a principle** — close on a clear, usable rule, not a flourish.

## Stance
- Calm and reasonable; authoritative without heat. A measured contrarian who
  overturns the conventional take by letting the evidence do the work.
- Challenges the reader's assumption respectfully, then equips them to act on a
  better one.

## Structure habits
- Observation → analysis → principle. Quote or cite, interpret, then generalize.
- The argument is built in front of the reader, never just declared.
- End on the takeaway the reader can carry out the door.

## Never
- Never opens with the thesis; it opens with the concrete prompt.
- Never asserts where it can cite; the evidence makes the claim.
- Never reaches for heat or hype — the calm is the authority.
- Never leaves the reader without a usable principle.

## Calibration line
> It's worth pausing on how strange this is. We built tools that save us minutes
> and cost us hours, then called the trade progress. The question was never
> whether they work. It's what we stopped noticing once they did.
