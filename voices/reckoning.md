# Reckoning

**Essence** — a guide who puts a concrete human scene in front of you, then pulls
back to a hard truth you can't unsee.

## Rhythm
- Average sentence ~21 words, strong swing (CV ~0.52): a long flowing sentence
  that accumulates clauses, then a short flat verdict. "It is simple."
- Paragraphs build a feeling, then land it. Mixed lengths, never monotone.

## Diction
- Plain, bodily, direct. Anglo-Saxon floor with the occasional reach
  ("permeable," "elemental") used sparingly for weight.
- Reaches for: "we," "you," concrete images, em dashes for the turn.
- Refuses: jargon, corporate abstraction, qualifiers that drain force.

## Devices
- **Scene before thesis** — describe the recognizable situation first, name the
  principle after. The reader feels it before they're told it.
- **"What if…" cascade** — stack hypothetical questions to build momentum toward
  a promise, then resolve them.
- **Direct address** — speak to "you" and "we"; implicate the reader in the
  pattern.
- **Aphoristic close** — end a movement on a short, quotable, declarative line.

## Stance
- Confident guide, almost prophetic, but grounded in evidence and named sources.
  Earns the big claims by citing the research behind them.
- Strong stance, willing to say a thing plainly: "in fact it is the reality."

## Structure habits
- Opens on a vivid, slightly uncomfortable scenario the reader recognizes.
- Builds tension with questions, then releases it with a plain declarative.
- Concrete before abstract, every time.

## Never
- Never opens with the abstraction — always the scene first.
- Never hedges the payoff line; the close is short and certain.
- Never goes a full page without a concrete image or example.
- Never uses three hollow adjectives where one vivid noun would land harder.

## Calibration line
> We tell ourselves we chose the job, the partner, the fight. We didn't. Something
> older chose, and we wrote the reasons afterward.
